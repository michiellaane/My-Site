Most digital projects don't fail because the team built them badly. They fail because nobody tested the idea first. Or they never get built at all, because the price tag killed the conversation. From idea to validated concept

Four phases, each with a clear output you own. You decide after every step whether to keep going.

  1. 01

    Discovery

    We map your assumptions, your users, and the competition. The goal is to get clear on what you know, and what you're only guessing at.

    Output Research brief
  2. 02

    Conceptualisation

    We turn what we learned into a concrete concept: who it's for, what it offers, and how the core interaction works. All of this before any design work starts.

    Output Concept document
  3. 03

    Prototype

    We build something people can actually click through, with just enough detail to get real reactions. It's fast, cheap, and meant to be thrown away.

    Output Clickable prototype
  4. 04

    Validation

    We put the prototype in front of real users and gather the evidence that either confirms the concept or points it in a better direction. No production code needed yet.

    Output Validation report
